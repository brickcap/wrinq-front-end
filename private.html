wrinq and privacy

Wrinq allows you to communicate privately to your friends, family or customers.You can send messages or engage in audio/video calls.Your privacy is ensured on several different levels.

First, wrinq collects very little information about you. When you sign up for the service you need to fill only two fields in the form. A username and a password. There is an optional third field that you can fill if you like which is your email address, just in case you forget your password and need to retreive it. That's it. It never asks or stores any other detail about you.

Any voice or video calls that you make through wrinq are unmonitored and take place over a peer to peer connection using web rtc.

Any messages that you send from wrinq to your contact are stored locally in your browser and the browser of the person to whom you have sent the message. Messages are not stored on wrinq's database ever.

Profiles are optional and should you choose to create one it will be stored right in your browser. Profiles are exchanged when you accept requests from other users. Again the exchange of profiles take place over a peer to peer connection using web rtc. Once exchanged the profile of your contact will also be stored locally in your browser. Like messages wrinq does not store any profile on it's database.

In short wrinq gives you a complete control of your data

Now I understand that what you are reading can be a bit hard to believe and I can understand the scepticism. To gain you trust I have open sourced 90% of wrinq. The messaging server that is resposible for routing calls is open sourced under the name wrinqle. The UI is open sourced as wrinqle-front-end. Please contribute and help me make wrinq successful.
